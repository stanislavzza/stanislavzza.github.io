It seems from Table 1 that accuracy declines as the quality measure improves, implying that it’s easier for judges to find agreement on lower-quality wines than higher-qualities ones. This may be a general effect, because I’ve seen it in other cases, but I have not done a literature review to find out more.

Notice that the bits per rating (log likelihood) decreases for the 3|4 cut-point because of the large value of \(p\), because there’s less uncertainty about the “guesses.”
